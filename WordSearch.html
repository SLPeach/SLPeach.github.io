This search finds regularly-spelled syllables with any of the following patterns:
  • Closed: (closed) or C. A syllable with a short vowel sound and at least one consonant at the end, as in act, belt, flick, prod, or jump.
  • Open: (open) or O. Long vowel sound at the end of the word, or followed by another syllable, as in able, we, I, go, or flu.
  • Silent-e: (silent_e), S, or E. Long vowel sound, followed by a single consonant and an unpronounced e as in trade, here, quite, stone, rule, or use
  • Vowel Team: (vowel_team), V, or T. Long vowel sound, matching the first written vowel, followed by another vowel, including y, as in way, three, lie, hoax, fruit, or due.
  • R-controlled: (r_controlled) or R: /or/ as in orchid, /ar/ as in art, and /er/ as in herb, irk, or urn.
  • Consonant-le: (consonant_le) or L: Following another syllable, a single consonant and the letters le, producing the consonant's sound and /uhl/ as in maple, trouble, or title.
For example:
  • (open)(closed) (whole word) matches ethos, frequent, and fluid.
  • (vowel_team)(consonant_le) (beginning of word) matches eagle, steeplechase and needled.
  • RE (end of word) matches moderate, perfume and Marlene.
